Welcome to my AI Detection Study

Hello! My name is Lukas Schaffrath, and I study Cognitive, Digital, and Empirical English Studies at RWTH Aachen University. As I was a teacher trainee for several years beforehand, and the topic of AI-generated texts has become increasingly relevant in education, I became interested in understanding how well university instructors can distinguish between human-written and AI-generated student texts. This study aims to explore this question and gather insights into the detectability of AI in academic writing.

In this study, you will be asked to classify a series of student essays as either AI-generated or human-written. Your task is to rely on your professional judgment and expertise in evaluating written texts.

You will be shown 10 excerpts from BA/MA student essays from RWTH Aachen University: 5 of them are generated by an AI and 5 are human-written. The order of presentation is randomized for each participant. The excerpts are 8 sentences long and from term papers on BA level.

Instructions

Before starting, please specify your demographic information in the form below, including your name, years of teaching experience at university level, and your department. Each form field is mandatory to start the study. Please note that this information is only used for research purposes and will remain confidential.

For each text, please indicate your classification and rate your confidence on a scale from 1 (very uncertain) to 5 (very confident). Take your time to read each text carefully; your response time will also be recorded for analysis.

Your participation is voluntary, and the responses are anonymous except for the basic demographic data you provide.
